# Church of Iomedae (Falcon's Hollow chapter)

> **Stub.** Created from the *Hollow's Last Hope* "Adventure
> Background" extract (`module:hlh:01-adventure-background`,
> decisions D-002 and D-004).

The local chapter of **Iomedae's faith** in Falcon's Hollow. The
modest building / chapel that serves as the town's primary
religious institution. Iomedae is described in the source extract
as **"goddess of valor, justice, and honor"** — preserved
verbatim per D-002 (already Remaster-compatible).

## Narrative role in PathSeeker

- **Where Gherman wakes up** (per *Vision* §4.3 V-Q10, retconned
  by D-004 from the earlier Sarenrae-chapel resolution).
- The Iomedaean clergy who recovered him from the Foam River
  shore are **stern, valor-coded, honor-bound** — antithetical to
  Gherman's apathetic-cynical baseline (V-Q8) and atheist stance
  (V-Q17).
- First-NPC interactions are loaded with **three-way tension**:
  - **Duty** (clergy attempting Iomedaean-coded compassion to a
    confusing outsider).
  - **Atheism** (Gherman refusing the divine on whose grace he
    technically depends for survival).
  - **Magnetism** (V-Q16 — individual clerics may feel the
    inappropriate pull toward Gherman despite their disapproval,
    producing private moral struggle).
- Their failed attempts to cure blackscour via prayer (per the
  source extract) are a **central setup beat** for chapter 2 —
  see deferred decision D-003 (`module:hlh:01-adventure-
  background`) for the in-fiction explanation framing.

## Local capacity (provisional)

Frontier-town chapter:
- **Modest staff** — a handful of clerics, mostly low rank.
- **Highest-rank cleric** present is likely rank 2–3 (cannot
  reach the *heal* tier needed to cure blackscour at scale).
  Pending confirmation when full HLH module roster imports.
- **Subordinate to** the wider Iomedaean church hierarchy, which
  is concentrated in Cheliax (historical), Lastwall (until its
  fall), and major cities.

## Aura / divine detection

Per V-Q16 (Aura) and V-Q17 (Atheist) interactions:
- **Iomedaean detection** of Gherman returns the **distinctive
  but ambiguous** Aura signal compounded with the **atheism-void**
  read — something present, something missing, neither matching
  any familiar Iomedaean category.
- The chapter's Iomedaean priests are the first NPCs to
  encounter this puzzle. Whether one of them becomes a recurring
  fixated investigator — companion, antagonist, fated-rival —
  is **open** (likely promotion candidate to Hero / MC NPC tier
  per the heroes-NPC roster TBD).
