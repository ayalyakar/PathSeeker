# DESIGN — Crime and Justice

> Living document. Crime detection, witnesses, guards, bounties, trials,
> prisons, executions, escape, vendettas, and the regional/legal layer.

---

## 1. Stance

If the world supports "do anything" (per Vision), it must also model
**consequences for crime**. Region-dependent law (Cheliax vs Andoran
vs Galt vs tribal), faction-modified, witness-based.

## 2. Crime Categories

- **Theft, burglary, pickpocketing**.
- **Assault, murder, manslaughter**.
- **Rape / sexual coercion** (per *NSFW*).
- **Smuggling, fraud, forgery**.
- **Vandalism, arson**.
- **Heresy, blasphemy** (region-specific).
- **Sedition, treason** (faction-specific).
- **Slavery** (legal in some regions, capital crime in others).
- **Witchcraft / magical crime** (region-specific).

## 3. Detection

- **Witnesses** — NPCs see crime; report based on personality and
  faction.
- **Forensic** (PF2e Survival/Society/Religion checks) — investigators
  follow trails.
- **Magical** (divinations) — modeled as PF2e RAW with clear
  counterplay.
- **Reputation propagation** (per WS-Q15).

## 4. Punishment

- **Bounty** (region/faction).
- **Arrest** + trial.
- **Imprisonment** (with breakout potential).
- **Corporal** (whipping, branding, mutilation — per *Body and
  Identity*).
- **Capital** (execution).
- **Slavery** (per N-Q9).
- **Religious** (excommunication per RD-Q21).

## 5. Cross-References

- *NPCs and Companions* — witnesses, guards.
- *Factions and Reputation* — faction reaction to crime.
- *World Simulation* — bounty propagation.

- *NSFW* — sexual crimes, slavery system, captivity.
