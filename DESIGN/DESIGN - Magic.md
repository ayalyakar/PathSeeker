# DESIGN — Magic

> Living document. Placeholder for the reworked magic system, which the
> Vision establishes as one of the largest mechanical departures from
> Golarion canon.

---

## 1. Stance

- **Full PF2e Remaster spell list**, plus additions, plus third-party
  spells, plus metamagic. Nothing is cut for breadth reasons.
- **Reworked thematically**: less esoteric, more **alchemical / rational**.
  Magic in this Golarion is closer to a discoverable natural science
  than to a numinous mystery.
- **Mage vs Fighter feel must be radically different.** Casting is not
  "press button, do damage" — it is its own cognitive surface.
- **High-quality UX/UI** for spellcasting, preparation, research, and
  metamagic. The magic interface is itself a feature of the game.

## 2. Implications of "Alchemical / Rational"

(Working hypotheses — to be designed thoroughly.)

- Spells have **components, costs, equations, and reagents**, not just
  slots. Casting a spell has chemistry-like steps.
- **Spell research and discovery** is a real activity (cf. side-activity
  list in *Core Loop and Structure*).
- **Metamagic** is composable transformation of a base spell, surfaced
  via UI rather than discrete feats.
- **Schools / Traditions / Essences** (Remaster's matter / mind / spirit
  / vital) are reframed as observable energy types rather than
  mystical categories.
- **Failure modes** are real and meaningful: misfire, backlash,
  contamination, residue.
- **Casters have a workshop / lab** — equivalent in importance to a
  fighter's armory.

## 4. Open Questions (Magic-Local)

- M-Q1. Spell preparation — preserved (spellbook, daily prep) or
  reshaped into a study/research loop?
- M-Q2. Spell slots — kept as resource axis, or replaced with a single
  resource (mana/focus/essence)?
- M-Q3. Reagents — universally required for all magic, or only ritual /
  high-tier?
- M-Q4. Spontaneous vs prepared casters — how does the rework treat the
  distinction?
- M-Q5. Divine magic — does "rational" framing imply divine becomes
  transactional/contractual rather than faith-based?
- M-Q6. Occult magic — leans hardest into "less esoteric" — what
  flavor remains?
- M-Q7. Innate / ancestry magic — preserved as biological?
- M-Q8. Rituals — full multi-actor, time-cost rituals supported as
  real diegetic events (multi-NPC, candles, circles, hours)?
- M-Q9. Counterspell / dispel — real-time handling.
- M-Q10. Spell research / invention — player-authored spells via the
  alchemical-rational system?
- M-Q11. Magic items — same rework, or magic-items-as-tech?
- M-Q12. Identification — *identify magic* as a player-skill activity.
