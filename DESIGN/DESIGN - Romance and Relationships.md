# DESIGN — Romance and Relationships

> Living document. The emotional layer of human connection in the
> game. Sits *adjacent to* but not *the same as* the explicit-content
> layer (which lives in *NSFW*). Heavily coupled to *NPCs and
> Companions*, *Body and Identity*, *Dialogue and Conversation*, and
> *Religion and Devotion*.

---

## 1. Stance

Relationships in this Golarion are:
- **Multi-modal**: friendship, mentorship, rivalry, romance,
  monogamous love, polyamorous love, casual sex, professional
  intimacy (sex work), abusive bonds, transactional marriages,
  spiritual unions.
- **Emergent**, not gated by single "approval" axes.
- **Time-grown**. A meaningful relationship takes weeks to months of
  in-game time (1 day = 3 real hours).
- **Lifelike-bidirectional**. NPCs pursue relationships with each
  other independent of the player (per N-Q7 / NPC-Q13).
- **Risky**. Heartbreak, jealousy, cuckoldry, betrayal, loss,
  divorce, widowhood are real and modeled.

## 2. Distinction from NSFW

- **Romance** = the emotional, narrative, and time-invested layer.
  Many relationships never become explicit (per N-Q14).
- **NSFW** (per *NSFW*) = the explicit, body, and intimate-scene
  layer. Many intimate scenes are not "romance" (transactional,
  coercive, casual).
- The two layers **interact constantly** but are designed
  separately so each is rich.

## 3. Relationship Model (proposed)

Each NPC↔X (where X may be the player or another NPC) carries:
- A **bond** type (friend, lover, enemy, mentor, rival, family, …).
- A multidimensional **affect map** (likes, loves, trusts, fears,
  resents, lusts after, idolizes, pities, …).
- A **history** (shared events, gifts, betrayals, intimate moments).
- **Public/private** state (some NPCs hide their feelings).

This replaces the BG3-style single approval axis with a richer
graph (per NPC-Q12).

## 5. Open Questions (Romance and Relationships-Local)

- **RR-Q1** Affect-map dimensions — propose a fixed schema (e.g.,
  Trust, Lust, Respect, Familiarity, Fear, Resentment, Devotion)
  vs free-form per NPC?
- **RR-Q2** Romance gates — what events advance / regress / break a
  romance? (Time spent, gifts, shared danger, sexual contact,
  betrayal, gifts given to others, public flirtation, secrets
  kept/broken, …)
- **RR-Q3** Polyamory model — explicit poly relationships (with
  metamours, jealousy curves), open relationships, or only sequential
  mono unless context-specified?
- **RR-Q4** Marriage — first-class institution? Per-region rules
  (Cheliax law vs Andoran vs tribal)?
- **RR-Q5** Children of relationships — gestation, naming, raising?
  Connects to L-Q5/L-Q6 (aging) and N-Q4.
- **RR-Q6** Companion-companion romance — fully simulated?
  Player-influenced, or autonomous?
- **RR-Q7** NPC-NPC romance off-screen — reported via gossip?
  Discovered by witnessing?
- **RR-Q8** Same-deity / cross-faith couples — religious tensions
  modeled?
- **RR-Q9** Cross-ancestry romance — modeled at scale (lifespan
  mismatches matter — a human-elf romance has tragic shape)?
- **RR-Q10** Heartbreak as a system — modeled as a mood/condition
  with consequences (depression debuff, comfort-seeking behavior,
  rebound risk)?
- **RR-Q11** Jealousy mechanics — NPC reaction to seeing partner
  with another?
- **RR-Q12** Public/private displays of affection — region/faction
  acceptability rules?
- **RR-Q13** Courtship rituals — region-specific (Tian courtly
  poetry, Shoanti vow-stones, Chelish formal contracts, …)?
- **RR-Q14** Gifts — meaningful per-NPC preferences, or generic
  affection-up?
- **RR-Q15** Shared activities (per Persona social links) — cooking,
  training, traveling, studying — bond-building events?
- **RR-Q16** "Compatible" vs "incompatible" — does the system
  pre-judge whether a romance can flourish, or let the player try?
- **RR-Q17** Romance with the iconics (NPC-Q17) — fully supported?
- **RR-Q18** Romance with deity avatars — possible?
- **RR-Q19** Romance with non-humanoids (dragons, fey, elementals,
  oozes) — supported under what constraints?
- **RR-Q20** Friendship-only progression — same depth?
- **RR-Q21** Mentorship — the player can mentor or be mentored, with
  long-time-investment payoffs?
- **RR-Q22** Rivalry — first-class arc type with its own mechanics
  (escalation, friendly competition, nemesis-becomes-friend)?
- **RR-Q23** Family relationships — the player accumulates "family"
  (bio, chosen, legal) and these have their own dynamics?
- **RR-Q24** Aging within relationships — partner ages with player?
  Differently per ancestry?
- **RR-Q25** Death of a loved NPC — explicit mourning system?
  Funerals as events?
