# DESIGN — Lore Reworks

> Living document. The **decision register** of every place we depart
> from Paizo upstream Golarion canon. Pairs with `Lore/Lore - Reworks.md`
> which holds the **content** of those reworks.
>
> *This doc* = "what we changed and why."
> *Lore folder* = "the canon as it now reads in our world."

---

## 1. Stance

We start from Golarion canonical and **rework heavily where the canon
under-serves the project's vision**, especially:

- The **alignment system removal** (resolved R-Q3) — the largest
  single rework; affects deities, factions, ancestries, the planes,
  spells, items.
- The **magic system rework** to alchemical/rational (per *Magic*) —
  affects cosmology, deity portfolios, school flavor, magical
  industry, magic items.
- **Mature treatment of sexuality** (per *NSFW*) — Calistria, Shelyn,
  Lamashtu, Norgorber, Urgathoa portfolios get reframed; Pharasma's
  attitude toward conception/birth/death is detailed; cult practices
  modeled with full fidelity.

Everything else stays close to Paizo canon by default.

## 2. Confirmed Reworks (this turn)

### LR-001 — Alignment removal (resolved R-Q3)

- **Source canon**: PF1e/pre-Remaster 9-axis alignment.
- **Our state**: Wholesale Honor Remaster — no alignment.
- **Affects**: deities, planes, fiends/celestials, anti-X effects,
  spells (e.g., *protection from evil* → *protection from evil
  trait* → *protection from spirits / fiends / void* per Remaster
  re-shape), ancestries that carried alignment associations
  (axiomatic dwarves, etc.), iconic NPC blurbs.
- **Replacement axes**: trait-targeting (`holy`, `unholy`,
  `sanctified`, `void`, `vitality`); deity edicts/anathema;
  faction values.
- **Open**: see LR-Q* below for spillover decisions.

### LR-002 — Magic as alchemical/rational (per *Magic*)

- **Source canon**: PF2e magic is largely esoteric / mystical /
  numinous; spells are described as taps on cosmological forces.
- **Our state**: Magic is a **discoverable natural science** with
  reagent-based casting (M-Q2 resolved: slots + reagents).
  Schools, traditions, and essences become observable energy
  classes rather than mysteries.
- **Affects**: school flavor, casting interface, ritual handling,
  divine magic framing, magical industry/economy, scholar
  professions, university institutions (e.g., Magaambya,
  Acadamae) get re-flavored.
- **Open**: M-Q1, M-Q3–M-Q12; see *Magic*.

### LR-003 — Gods (per *Religion and Devotion*)

- **Source canon**: Golarion deities are described in alignment-coded,
  metaphysically-mystical terms; their power source is generally
  treated as numinous and beyond mortal understanding.
- **Our state**: Deities are **empirically-detectable entities**
  with measurable power signatures. Worship is a **relationship**
  (often transactional, sometimes loving, sometimes contractual,
  sometimes coerced) rather than a metaphysical given. Edicts and
  anathema (per Remaster) are the operative axes — not alignment.
  Divine magic is not different in *kind* from arcane/primal/occult
  under the rational rework, but its **source** is a deity contract.
- **Affects**: every deity blurb, divine spellcasting flavor,
  Boneyard/judgment mechanics, planar coherence (per LR-Q5–LR-Q8),
  religious factions, sacred-rite design (sex, pain, secrets,
  death), Calistria/Shelyn/Lamashtu/Pharasma/Urgathoa/Norgorber/
  Zon-Kuthon/Cayden-Cailean portfolios all reframed for mature
  treatment.
- **Implication for Gherman**: his first NPC encounter is at
  **Sarenrae's chapel** (per *Vision* §4.3). Sarenrae is therefore
  the first deity-as-entity Gherman meets the influence of —
  cleric/healer NPC voice, healing-as-divine-mechanism on display,
  potential opening religious lever (regardless of where Gherman
  ultimately stands).
- **Open**: see *Religion and Devotion* RD-Q* (full slate).

### LR-004 — Meta Lore (cosmology, history, planes, the Great Beyond)

- **Source canon**: Golarion's macro-history, cosmology, planar
  structure, the Inner/Outer Sphere, the Great Beyond, Earthfall,
  the Age of Lost Omens, the Worldwound, Aroden's death,
  Iomedae's ascension, etc.
- **Our state**: Nominally canonical at the macro-history layer, but
  **the cosmology and planar structure are reworked to be coherent
  without alignment** (per LR-001). Specifics are dependent on
  LR-Q5–LR-Q8 resolutions, but the design intent is:
  - **Planes** regrouped by trait/theme (per LR-Q5) rather than
    alignment quadrant.
  - **The Boneyard / Pharasma** still sorts souls — but by what
    measurable axis (LR-Q6).
  - **Outsiders** (devils, demons, angels, archons, agathions,
    azata, daemons, kytons, psychopomps) re-traited rather than
    re-aligned.
  - **Divine source** under "rational magic" is empirical
    (deities are observable contractable entities — see LR-003).
  - **Faith vs reason** as a meaningful in-fiction axis: Rahadoumi
    Laws of Mortality (atheism) becomes a more coherent stance in
    a Golarion where deities are observable rather than metaphysical
    givens.
  - **Gherman's isekai origin** (per *Vision* §4.2) implicates the
    cosmology — at some point we must decide whether the source
    world is a Golarion plane, a separate plane, or fully outside
    the Great Beyond entirely (V-Q3-followup).
- **Affects**: every plane page, every deity page, the Boneyard /
  judgment design, outsider taxonomies, Aroden / Worldwound /
  Mendev arcs, isekai cosmology.
- **Open**: LR-Q5–LR-Q8 plus the new LR-Q below.

## 3. Reworks To Decide (proposed scope)

These are areas where the project's vision creates pressure on the
canon. Not all need reworking — listed for the user to review and
decide which to engage.

### Cosmology and the divine

- The **Outer Sphere** (Heaven, Hell, Abaddon, Abyss, Nirvana, etc.)
  was alignment-coded. With alignment gone, what holds the planes
  together? Pure traits (sanctified/unholy)? Themes (order,
  freedom, balance, predation)? Existential modes?
- The **Boneyard** and **Pharasma's judgment** — what does it mean to
  judge a soul without alignment?
- The **deities** — every deity blurb has alignment-coded language.
  Replace with edicts/anathema (already started in Remaster) plus
  values.
- **Faith vs reason** — under alchemical magic, is divine magic just
  "the contract you signed with this entity"? What does worship
  look like in this Golarion?

### Deities (called-out portfolios)

- **Calistria** — lust/revenge. Sexuality fully matures; rituals
  modeled (per N-Q11).
- **Shelyn** — love/beauty/art. Romance fully matures.
- **Lamashtu** — monstrous birth. Pregnancy/birth/transformation
  fully matures (per *Body and Identity*).
- **Pharasma** — birth/death. Conception, miscarriage, birth, death,
  and afterlife as serious systems.
- **Urgathoa** — gluttony/disease/undeath. Food, drink, deviance,
  consumption modeled.
- **Norgorber** — secrets/murder/poison/greed. Crime fully modeled.
- **Zon-Kuthon** — pain/darkness. Body modification, BDSM,
  consensual and non-consensual pain.
- **Cayden Cailean** — alcohol/freedom. Drinking, drunkenness,
  carousing.

### Factions

- Most factions are minimally rework-pressured. Specific cases:
  - **Hellknights** — alignment-coded discipline; reframe via
    edicts/values, not "lawful evil."
  - **Aspis Consortium** — fine.
  - **Eagle Knights** — fine.
  - **Magaambya / Acadamae** — re-flavored under alchemical magic.
  - **Whispering Way** — necromantic; aligns with Urgathoa rework.

### Ancestries

- Those carrying alignment baggage (e.g., the old
  "axiomatic dwarves," "chaotic-flavored elves") simply lose that
  language — Remaster already does this.
- Body/anatomy detail per ancestry needed for NSFW (per *NSFW*) —
  this is **content**, not a "rework" per se, but it's a place
  where canonical Golarion is silent and we fill in.

### History

- Earthfall, Age of Lost Omens: probably stays.
- Iomedae's ascension and the Mendevian Crusades: stay.
- Worldwound closure: stay.

### Setting fixtures

- **Magic items as industrial products**: under alchemical magic,
  magic items are factory-produced more than mystically forged.
  Affects *Crafting*, *Economy*.
- **Pathfinder Society** as scholarly/exploratory adventuring guild
  — fine; minimal rework.

### Player-protagonist-specific

- Per V-Q1, Gherman's origin is canonically unknown. The
  "in-fiction why" must be authored: amnesia, exile, ritual, etc.
  This may require **inserting** a small piece of new canon
  (a place, a person, an event) into Golarion's history. Per
  *Workflow*, every such insertion will be Q&A'd before it's
  written.
